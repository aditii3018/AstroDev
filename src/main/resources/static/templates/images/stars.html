Explore the Stars

Learn about different stars and their characteristics!

What Are Stars?

Stars are massive celestial bodies made of gas that emit light and heat. They are held together by gravity and undergo nuclear fusion, which produces energy.

Types of Stars

Red Dwarfs: Small, cool stars that are the most common in the universe.

Yellow Dwarfs: Like our Sun, these stars are medium-sized and have a longer lifespan.

Blue Giants: Massive and hot stars that burn their fuel quickly.

Supernovae: Explosive end stages of massive stars, resulting in a brilliant burst of light.

Famous Stars

Here are some well-known stars that you might have heard about:

Sirius: The brightest star in the night sky, located in the Canis Major constellation.

Betelgeuse: A red supergiant star in the Orion constellation, known for its distinctive color.

Polaris: The North Star, located nearly directly above the North Pole.

Rigel: A blue supergiant star in the Orion constellation, one of the most luminous stars known.

Fun Facts About Stars

There are over 100 billion stars in our galaxy alone.

The closest star to Earth (other than the Sun) is Proxima Centauri, about 4.24 light-years away.

Stars can be born, live for billions of years, and die in spectacular explosions.
